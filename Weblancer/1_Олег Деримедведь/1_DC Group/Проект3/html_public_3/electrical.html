PAT

A safety PAT (Portable Appliance Testing) check-up is a workplace examination of electrical equipment. The test identifies whether the equipment in your workplace is safe to be used on a day to day basis.

You have a responsibility as an employer to adhere to ‘The Electricity at Work Regulations 1989’ statute, which states that any electrical equipment that could cause injury must be maintained in a safe condition. Although there is no official law to specify when you should have an inspection, it is advised you schedule a regular check-up as both the environment and the type of equipment can vary massively.

Our inspection will cover your entire electrical catalogue – providing a comprehensive report at the end highlighting any issues you might have. Schedule a visit today!

FIXED WIRE TESTING

Fixed wire testing is a popular way of describing Electrical Installation Condition Reports (EICR). Simply put, fixed wiring investigates and tests electrical services & any system that conducts electricity in a building, to identify compliance. Unlike a PAT test though, fixed wiring assesses the internal wiring of a building (meticulously in order to assure accuracy).

Any electrical installation will degrade over time; regular tests are therefore a must in order to be legally compliant. Deficiencies can be identified in your electrical system and handled before causing you time, money and frustration (as well as alleviating the risk of fire and injury).

Our team of expert electricians are here to help, so get in touch today.
